ITC Ltd (NSE: ITC) is one of India's most admired and valuable companies known for its strong presence across businesses like cigarettes, hotels, paperboards, packaging, agri-business and FMCG products. ITC has a track record of delivering strong financial performance, maintaining leadership across its key businesses, and creating significant shareholder value over the past decades.

 

In this article, we take a deep dive into ITC's historical financials, analyze its performance across key parameters, and evaluate the stock's current valuation to assess its investment merit. 

 

ITC Financial Analysis

 

Revenue Growth

 

Over the past 10 years, ITC has delivered a healthy compounded annual growth rate (CAGR) of 8.1% in revenues. Revenue grew from Rs 35,306 crore in FY2014 to Rs 70,919 crore in FY2023. The acceleration in growth is even more visible in the recent past - ITC's revenues have grown at 12.8% CAGR over the last 3 years compared to 8.8% CAGR over the past 7 years. This indicates the company's strong brand equity and pricing power across its various product segments.

 

The standout segment has been the FMCG business which has grown at a 20%+ CAGR in the past decade and now contributes over 25% to ITC's revenues. The hotels business has also reported strong growth driven by new property additions. While the cigarettes business remains the dominant contributor at over 40% share, ITC has managed to successfully diversify its revenue streams over the years.

 

Profitability  

 

ITC's profitability levels and margins are among the best in the Indian corporate universe. The company has delivered an operating profit CAGR of 8.9% over the past 10 years. Despite revenue growth acceleration, operating margins have remained extremely stable in the 36-38% range highlighting exemplary cost management. 

 

ITC has maintained gross margins in the range of 40-42% over the past decade. However, rising competitive intensity in the FMCG segment has led to increase in advertising & promotion costs impacting EBITDA margins slightly. Still, the stability demonstrates ITC's ability to exercise pricing power across its product portfolio. 

 

Return on equity (ROE) has moderated from 33% in 2014 to 28% in 2023. This is largely due to ITC's aggressive diversification strategy over the years which has led to higher capital employed in new businesses thereby impacting ROE. However, 28% ROCE is still healthy for a diversified conglomerate. ROCE has also been range-bound around 34-35% reflecting efficient capital allocation.

 

Overall, ITC's profitability profile remains best-in-class reflecting its dominant market position across segments, prudent cost management and ability to charge premium pricing.

 

Financial Health

 

ITC maintains one of the strongest balance sheets in corporate India with negligible debt levels. ITC's reserves have grown steadily from Rs 26,441 crore in FY2014 to Rs 67,912 crore in FY2023 indicating strong and consistent profit retention. 

 

Borrowings remain extremely low at just Rs 306 crore as on FY2023. In contrast, cash & investments stand at a healthy Rs 29,415 crore highlighting the company's rock solid financial position. ITC has generated strong operating cash flows year-after-year enabling high investments as well as high shareholder payouts without compromising balance sheet strength.

 

Working capital management is another huge positive for ITC. Despite the growth in business scale, ITC has maintained negative working capital reflecting highly efficient inventory and receivables management. Average receivables days is around 15-20 days while average payables period is 40-45 days. This gives ITC access to substantial free cash flows.

 

Overall, ITC boasts one of the strongest balance sheets not just in the FMCG space but across sectors with no leverage, robust cash reserves and flawless working capital management.

 

Cash Flows 

 

A strong operating profit profile has translated into steady growth in operating cash flows over the years. ITC has generated robust annual operating cash flows (OCF) of Rs 18,800 crore in FY2023. The 10 year OCF CAGR stands at ~10% reflective of stable profit growth. 

 

However, higher investments especially in the hotels & paperboards business has meant increased capital expenditure. As a result, free cash flows (FCF) generation has been volatile for ITC. But the company has largely been able to fund its expansion plans through internal accruals without raising debt. On average, ITC has delivered FCF of ~Rs 5,000-6,000 crore per year over the past decade.

 

With large expansion plans behind, ITC's capex intensity is expected to ease. Given the high OCF generation ability, ITC should be able to deliver strong FCFs in the range of Rs 8,000-10,000 crore per year for the foreseeable future. Overall, ITC has demonstrated disciplined approach to managing cash flows and shareholder returns while maintaining balance sheet strength.

 

Valuation

 

The ITC stock has generated 18% CAGR returns over the past 10 years significantly outperforming the broader Nifty which delivered 11% CAGR. However, returns have moderated in the past 3-5 years as revenue and profit growth decelerated before recovering recently. 

 

ITC currently trades at a P/E multiple of 28x based on FY2023 earnings. This is towards the higher end of its 10 year average P/E of 25x. While the valuation looks expensive compared to its long term trading range, it is important to note ITC's earnings growth acceleration over the past 3 years.

 

With revenue growth at ~13%, operating profits growing at ~17% and ROE expansion to 28%, ITC has entered a high growth phase. The stock's premium valuation is factoring in continuation of this growth momentum. Compared to FMCG peers like HUL, Nestle which trade upwards of 50-60x P/E, ITC is relatively undervalued considering its strong brand portfolio and growth prospects.

 

Overall, while ITC's valuation appears stretched in the near term, the company's long growth runway across cigarettes, FMCG, hotels and agri-business segments should support premium valuations in the long run. Investors would do well to take advantage of any corrections to build exposure to this marquee compounder.

 

Final Thoughts

 

To summarize, here are the key positives for ITC Ltd:

 

- Leadership across high potential industries - cigarettes, hotels, paperboards, FMCG

- Strong brand equity ensures pricing power and margins stability 

- Excellent balance sheet with no leverage and robust cash reserves

- Stellar track record of managing costs, working capital and delivering stable growth

- Acceleration in revenue growth and profitability over the past 3 years

- Reasonable valuation considering growth prospects and return ratios

 

ITC's business fundamentals appear stronger than ever today. With a more focused approach on the FMCG opportunity, growth prospects look healthy. While valuations appear stretched in the near term after the recent run-up, long term investors would do well to accumulate the stock on dips for an opportunity to own this marquee compounder. ITC remains a core portfolio stock to hold for the long run.
